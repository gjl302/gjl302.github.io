Gustav J.W. Lundberg

Gustav J.W. Lundberg is a first year PhD student in the Social Psychology program at NYU, working with Jonathan B. Freeman in the Social Cognitive & Neural Sciences Lab. He is interested in the neural mechanisms underlying stereotypes, intergroup perception, and social categorization. His past research has examined how age interacts with racial stereotypes.

  • Learn more

Gustav J.W. Lundberg recieved his B.A. in Psychology and Sociology from the University of Iowa in 2017. He completed his honors thesis in Psychology with Dr. Rebecca Neel and Dr. Andrew Todd, examining how racial bias in implicit danger associations generalizes to older male targets. He completed his honors thesis in Sociology with Dr. Alison Bianchi, examining how gender shapes belief and non-belief using data from the Worlds Values Survey.
